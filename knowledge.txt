Definition:
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that combines information retrieval with generative language models.

Problem:
Large language models sometimes generate incorrect or outdated information because they rely only on internal training data.

How Retrieval Works:
RAG retrieves relevant documents from an external knowledge base before generating an answer.

Context Injection:
The retrieved documents are provided as context to the language model.

Benefits:
RAG improves factual accuracy and allows models to use external, updated information.

Architecture:
A typical RAG pipeline includes document storage, vectorization, similarity search, and response generation.

Embeddings:
Text is converted into numerical vectors called embeddings to measure similarity.

Similarity Search:
Cosine similarity is commonly used to find the most relevant documents.

Use Cases:
RAG is used in chatbots, search systems, enterprise knowledge assistants, and question-answering systems.
